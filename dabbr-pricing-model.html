Real upside

UPI Autopay take-rate — the second revenue line

Subscription is the floor. The real money is sitting in payment volume: a tiffin operator with 100 customers at ₹3,000/month each does ₹3 lakh of GMV. A 0.5% Dabbr take-rate on auto-collected payments is more revenue than the subscription itself — and it's nearly pure margin because UPI per-transaction costs are minimal (often zero, capped by NPCI rules).

Strategy

Five pricing decisions that follow from this model

1. Don't match TiffinCRM at ₹199 — undercut at ₹149

A 25% discount on the perceived market price gives operators a clean "switch" reason. You still have 50% gross margin at Growth tier.

2. Design every flow to keep customers in the 24h window

One free reply > 5 paid templates. Train customers to message first ("WhatsApp YES to confirm tomorrow's lunch"). Each percentage point of free-window utilisation is real margin.

3. Make UPI Autopay the headline feature of Business tier

It's the lock-in. Once an operator switches to UPI Autopay mandates collected via Dabbr, churning means re-collecting consent from every customer. Sticky.

4. Lead with AiSensy or MSG91, not Gupshup

AiSensy starts ~₹999/mo platform fee. MSG91 passes through at cost. Gupshup is enterprise pricing. Switch later if you outgrow them.

5. Don't introduce per-customer pricing

Operators can't predict customer count and will fear the bill. Bucket-based tiers are easier to sell. If you must meter, meter on WhatsApp messages, not customers.

6. Build the BSP relationship before the API

WhatsApp Business API approval can take 2–6 weeks. Apply for the green tick + Meta Business verification now, even if the integration ships in 3 months.

Sources & assumptions

Where the numbers came from

WhatsApp pricing (India, 2026): AiSensy · MessageBot · 2factor · TeleCRM. Utility messages: ₹0.13–0.145 per delivered message after July 2025 per-template-message pricing change.
BSP platform fees: AiSensy ₹999/mo entry, Interakt ₹999/mo, MSG91 platform-fee + pass-through model. Used ₹2,000/mo as conservative midpoint for share allocation.
UPI economics: NPCI-mandated zero MDR on UPI P2P/P2M for transactions ≤ ₹2,000; merchant fees regulated. Dabbr's 0.5% take-rate would be a Dabbr-charged convenience fee on top of UPI rails, not an MDR.
Competitor anchor: TiffinCRM ₹199/mo for ≤30 orders/day · Dinespot ~$49 CAD/mo (~₹3,000).
All margin figures are gross — they exclude customer acquisition, support staff, and product engineering costs.
